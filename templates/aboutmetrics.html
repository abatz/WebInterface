The above image shows the NDVI anomaly in the U.S. for August 1993. In that year, heavy rain in the Northern Great Plains (North and South Dakota, Alberta, and Saskatchewan) led to flooding in the Missouri River. The resulting exceptionally lush vegetation appears as a positive anomaly (green). Concurrently, in the Eastern U.S., rainfall was very low, and the region exhibited a strong negative anomaly (dark red). (Credit: http://earthobservatory.nasa.gov/)
